Crispy Chicken w/Potato Crust

Serves 4

Ingredients

  • 1 lgebaking potato about 8 oz. peeled
  • 3 tbsDijon-style mustard
  • 1 clovegarlic minced
  • 1/4 tspdried thyme leaves
  • 4boneless, skinless chicken breast halves
  • non-stick cooking spray
  • 1 1/2 tspolive oil
  • ground black pepper to taste
  • 1/4 tspsalt
  • 2 tbsfinely chopped fresh parsley or green onions

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 425 degrees.
  2. Shred the potato through the largest holes of a cheese grater.
  3. Transfer shredded potato to a bowl of ice water and soak 5 minutes.
  4. While potato is soaking, wash chicken and pat dry. Mix mustard, garlic and thyme in a small bawl. Using a spatula or a spoon, spread the mixture evenly on the top side of each chicken breast half. Fold the thin end of the breasts under to prevent them from overcooking.
  5. Coat a baking dish with non-stick cooking spray. Arrange chicken, mustard-side up in the dish.
  6. Drain the potato. Blot dry with paper towels. In a large bowl, mix potato, olive oil, pepper and salt. Spread each chicken breast with about about a 1/2 cup of the potato mixture forming an even crust.
  7. Bake 30 to 35 minutes, on an upper rack in the oven
  8. Chicken should be firm to the touch and the potato should be golden. (If potatoes do not brown, broil about 3 minutes under the oven broiler)
  9. Sprinkle with parsley or chopped green onions. Serve hot with steamed carrots or broccoli. Serves 4
