Brine Chicken

Photo: Lexlex (CC BY-SA 4.0)

Chicken breast halves soaked in a salt and brown sugar brine seasoned with peppercorns, chili pepper, bay leaves, lemon, onion, and garlic, then grilled.

Ingredients

  • 4 6-8 ozchicken breasts, halves
  • 1/4 cupcourse salt
  • 1/4 cupfirmly packed dark brown sugar
  • 12 wholeblack peppercorns
  • 1red chilly pepper thinly sliced
  • 2 wholebay leaves
  • 1 cuphot water
  • 3 cupscold water
  • 1lemon sliced thinly
  • 1 smonion thinly sliced
  • 2 clovesgarlic, minced

Directions

  1. Rinse chicken under cold, running water and blot dry with paper towels. Arrange in a noncreative baking dish.
  2. Make the brine: Place the salt, brown sugar, peppercorns, chili pepper and bay leaves in a large, noncreative bowl, Add the hot water, then whisk until the salt and brown sugar dissolve; stir in cold water. Let brine come to room temperature. Pour over the chicken and stir in the lemon slices, onion and garlic. Cover the dish with plastic wrap and let stand in the refrigerator for 2 hours, turning the chicken once or twice.
  3. Preheat the grill until coals are hot. When ready to cook, brush and oil the grate. Arrange the breasts on the hot grate, all facing the same direction, at a 45 degree angle to the bars of the grate. Cook the chicken over high heat, 4 to 6 minutes per side, rotating the breasts 90 degrees after 2 minutes on each side to create an attractive crosshatch of grill marks.
