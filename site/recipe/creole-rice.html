Creole Rice

Photo: aimeeorleans (CC BY-SA 2.0)

A Creole-style rice simmered in tomato sauce with onion, garlic, red bell pepper, and seasonings of oregano, thyme, cayenne, and bay leaves.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsolive oil
  • 1chopped onion
  • 3 clovesgarlic, finely chopped
  • 1 cupchopped red bell peppers
  • 1 tspfried oregano leaves, crushed
  • 1 tspsalt
  • 3/4 tspdried thyme leaves crushed
  • 1/4 tspcayenne pepper
  • 2bay leaves
  • 1 can15 oz. tomato sauce
  • 1 can14 1/2 oz chopped tomatoes and juice
  • 1/2 cupwater
  • 2 cupslong grain rice

Directions

  1. In a medium saucepan with cover, heat oil over medium high heat. Add onion and garlic. Saute for 4 to 5 minutes. Stir in bell pepper, oregano, salt, thyme, and cayenne. Cook for 1 minute. Add bay leaves, tomato sauce and water; bring to a boil. Stir in rice, cover and reduce heat to low. Cook for 20 minutes or until rice is tender and liquid is absorbed. Let stand covered for 5 minutes. Remove bay leaves.
  1. In a medium saucepan with a cover, heat the oil over medium-high heat. Add the onion and garlic and saute for 4 to 5 minutes.
  2. Stir in the bell pepper, oregano, salt, thyme, and cayenne. Cook for 1 minute.
  3. Add the bay leaves, tomato sauce, and water; bring to a boil.
  4. Stir in the rice, cover, and reduce heat to low. Cook for 20 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed.
  5. Let stand, covered, for 5 minutes. Remove the bay leaves.
