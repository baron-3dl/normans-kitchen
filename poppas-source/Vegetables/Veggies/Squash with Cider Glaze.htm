Squash with Cider Glaze
 
 
2   acorn squash, halved and seeded  
1 1/2 cups    apple cider, or apple juice  
1 cup    water  
 
2 tsp    balsamic vinegar  
  salt and pepper to taste  
 
1 Heat oven to 350 degrees. Into an oven proof baking dish that will comfortably hold squash, pour in enough water to reach a dept of 1 inch. Place squash halves cut side down in dish, Bake 15 minutes.
2 In a nonstick saucepan, combine cider, 1 cup water and vinegar. bring to a boil over high heat and, stirring occasionally, allow mixture to reduce to about half, about 12 minutes.
3 Remove squash from oven. Carefully turn cut-side up with tongs or tablespoons. Using fork,prick interior all over. With a small brush, coat inside of squash wit some of the cider mixture. Lightly sprinkle with salt and pepper. Return squash to oven to continue baking. Repeat brushing on cider mixture every 10 to 15 minutes until squash is almost fork tender, about 60 to 75 minutes, depending on oven and size of squash.
4 During the last minutes of baking; cook cider mixture over high heat until reduced to a thick, glazelike sauce, 5 minutes. When squash is fork tender, remove from oven. Carefully transfer to platter or plates. Brush squash with glaze, allowing a little to pool in the bottom of each cavity.
